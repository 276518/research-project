Solar System

This is our solar system. A solar system is a collection of planets, moons, and asteriods that orbit a star.

Spirit

Spirit is a mars rover that was active from 2004-2010. This rover was one of the twin rovers, the other was opportunity. They were famous due to being far more successful that expected.

Apollo 11

Apollo 11 was the the first ever spacecraft to successfully take people to the moon. The spacecraft took off on July 16, 1969 and landed on the moon four days later.

Neil Armstrong

Neil Armstrong was an astronaut aboard Apollo 11. He was the first man to ever take foot on the moon. He stepped out of the Apollo 11 lunar module and onto the Moon's surface, in an area called the Sea of Tranquility.

The Sun

In our solar system we have one star. It's called the sun. It is a massive, hot ball of plasma, inflated and heated by energy produced by reactions in it's core. The sun is so big that we could fit 330,000 Earths inside. We are about 93 million miles away from the sun but we can still feel and see it's rays. The sun is the center of our solar system and it is the object that the rest of the planets orbit around.

Mercury

Mercury is the first planet is our solar system and is also the smallest. Mercury is only slightly larger that Earth's moon. Mercury is classified as a terrestrial planet, meaning that is has a solid surface and is mainly made of silicate rocks or metals. Mercury's length of a day is 59 earth days! This means that is revolves slowly compared to Earth.

Venus

Venus is the second planet in our solar system and is known for having the most similar mass and size to Earth. Venus is also known for having the most dense atmosphere of the terrestrial planets, composed mostly of carbon dioxide. Venus's length of a day is 243 earth days. It's orbital period, or year, is 225 days meaning that there is less than one Venus day, in one Venus year!
